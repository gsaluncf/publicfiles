Lab 2: The Pizza Shop — A Live Messaging Exercise

This is not a solo lab. You will work as an entire class to operate a distributed pizza shop. Half of you are customers placing orders. The other half are cooks fulfilling them. Everyone connects to the same pair of SQS queues, and the only way you communicate is through messages.

How It Works

Your instructor has created two shared SQS queues for the class:

Queue Purpose Who Writes? Who Reads?
pizza-orders Incoming orders from customers Customers Cooks
pizza-results Completion confirmations from cooks Cooks Customers

Each order carries a Correlation ID — a unique ticket number. When a cook finishes, they post the result back to the response queue with that same ID. The customer polls the response queue looking for their specific ID. This is how a customer knows their pizza is done, even though dozens of customers are all sharing the same queue.

Instructor Setup (Not for Students)

Before class, create the two shared queues:

import boto3

sqs = boto3.client('sqs', region_name='us-east-1')

# Create order queue
orders = sqs.create_queue(
    QueueName='pizza-orders',
    Attributes={'VisibilityTimeout': '60', 'MessageRetentionPeriod': '3600'}
)
print(f"ORDER_QUEUE_URL = '{orders['QueueUrl']}'")

# Create response queue
results = sqs.create_queue(
    QueueName='pizza-results',
    Attributes={'VisibilityTimeout': '10', 'MessageRetentionPeriod': '3600'}
)
print(f"RESPONSE_QUEUE_URL = '{results['QueueUrl']}'")

# Share both URLs with students

After class, delete both queues:

sqs.delete_queue(QueueUrl=ORDER_QUEUE_URL)
sqs.delete_queue(QueueUrl=RESPONSE_QUEUE_URL)
print("Both queues deleted.")

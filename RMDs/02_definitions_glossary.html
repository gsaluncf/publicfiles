Glossary: Wealth Management Terms for Data Scientists

Formal definitions with data modeling implications

How to Use This Glossary

Each term includes a formal definition, a plain-language explanation, and notes on how the concept affects database design or simulation logic.

Core Tax Concepts

Term Formal Definition Data Modeling Note
Required Minimum Distribution (RMD) The minimum amount that must be withdrawn annually from tax-deferred retirement accounts starting at age 73, calculated as account balance divided by an IRS life expectancy factor. Requires annual recalculation. The divisor comes from IRS Uniform Lifetime Table. Must track account balance at year-end.
Step-Up in Basis Upon death, inherited assets receive a new cost basis equal to fair market value at date of death, eliminating all unrealized capital gains for tax purposes. Simulation must track death event and reset cost_basis = market_value at that moment. Affects terminal wealth calculation.
Cost Basis The original purchase price of an asset, used to calculate capital gains or losses when the asset is sold. Basis = Purchase Price + Adjustments. Immutable after purchase (except for specific adjustments). Stored per tax lot, not per security.
Unrealized Gain The difference between current market value and cost basis for an asset that has not been sold. Unrealized Gain = Market Value - Cost Basis. Computed field, not stored. Changes with every market price update. This is what Step-Up eliminates.
Tax Lot A record of a specific purchase of a security, including date, quantity, and price. Multiple purchases of the same security create multiple tax lots. Primary entity in our schema. One client may have hundreds of lots. Each lot has its own basis and holding period.
Tax Alpha The excess wealth generated by tax-optimized investment decisions compared to a tax-naive baseline strategy, measured in dollars or percentage points. Our target metric. Computed as E[Wealth|Strategy A] - E[Wealth|Baseline] across simulation paths.

Account Types

Account Type Tax Treatment RMD Required? Step-Up Applies?
Traditional IRA / 401(k) Tax-deferred. Contributions deductible, withdrawals taxed as ordinary income. Yes, starting age 73 No. Entire balance taxable to heirs.
Roth IRA / Roth 401(k) Tax-free growth. Contributions after-tax, qualified withdrawals tax-free. No (for original owner) N/A. Already tax-free.
Taxable Brokerage No special treatment. Dividends and realized gains taxed annually. No Yes. Heirs inherit at FMV.

Statistical / Simulation Terms

Term Definition
Geometric Brownian Motion (GBM) A stochastic process where the logarithm of the variable follows a Brownian motion with drift. Used to model stock prices: dS = μS dt + σS dW, where μ is drift (expected return), σ is volatility, and dW is a Wiener process increment.
Survival Function S(t) The probability that an individual survives beyond time t. S(t) = P(T > t), where T is the random variable representing time of death. Derived from actuarial life tables.
Monte Carlo Simulation A computational technique that uses repeated random sampling to obtain numerical results. We generate N independent paths, apply deterministic logic to each, then aggregate statistics across all paths.
Value at Risk (VaR) The maximum expected loss at a given confidence level over a specified time horizon. VaR(95%) = the 5th percentile of the terminal wealth distribution.
Decision Boundary The set of parameter values (e.g., volatility, life expectancy) where two strategies have equal expected outcomes. Above the boundary, Strategy A dominates; below, Strategy B dominates.

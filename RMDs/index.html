Welcome to the Wealth Management Data Seminar

This seminar uses a real wealth-management problem to learn data modeling, simulation, and decision science. Start here.

A) Wealth Management as a Business

Wealth management is a data-driven service business. Advisors translate client goals into portfolio, tax, and cash-flow decisions under constraints like risk tolerance, account types, and regulations. Every recommendation—asset allocation, rebalancing, withdrawals—relies on data feeds, a clear data model, and measurable outcomes.

  • Value chain: Intake (KYC) → Planning → Implementation → Monitoring → Reporting
  • Data flows: Holdings/tax lots, market data, tax rules, life events
  • Decisions: What to sell, when to realize gains, where to draw cash from, how to minimize taxes

B) Retirement and Tax Efficiency

In retirement, tax efficiency compounds. The sequence of withdrawals across account types (Traditional IRA, Roth, taxable) determines lifetime taxes paid and the wealth passed to heirs. The right sequence can create Tax Alpha—excess value generated purely from tax-aware choices.

  • Key levers: account location, realization timing, loss harvesting, conversion windows
  • Constraints: income brackets, IRMAA, capital-gain rates, estate rules
  • Outcome: higher after-tax wealth for the client and heirs

C) RMDs and the Step-Up — Our Running Example

Required Minimum Distributions (RMDs) force withdrawals (taxed as income) from tax-deferred accounts starting in your early 70s. Meanwhile, Step-Up in Basis forgives unrealized gains at death for taxable accounts. These rules pull in opposite directions:

  • RMDs say: withdraw sooner and pay taxes now.
  • Step-Up says: hold appreciated assets longer and potentially pay no capital gains.

Which path builds more multi-generational wealth? That depends on market returns and lifespan—both uncertain. This seminar uses RMDs vs Step-Up as a concrete lens to learn the data, logic, and analytics of wealth management.

D) From Concepts to Analysis

We will model uncertain markets and lifespans, encode tax logic in a data model of tax lots, and run Monte Carlo simulations to compare strategies. Your goal is to identify when the optimal strategy flips—and why.

  • Data model: represent accounts, lots, basis, and cash flows
  • Statistics: GBM for prices, survival distributions for mortality
  • Simulation: 104+ paths, aggregated to decision metrics

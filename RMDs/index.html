Wealth Management Data Seminar

A 1.5-day intensive for data science graduate students

What This Seminar Is About

This seminar teaches you how to work with financial data by solving a real problem: figuring out when a retiree should take money out of their retirement accounts versus letting it grow.

This is not a finance course. You will not become a financial advisor. Instead, you will learn:

  • How to model financial data in a database (tax lots, accounts, transactions)
  • How to simulate uncertain futures using Monte Carlo methods
  • How to turn simulation results into decisions using SQL aggregation

The wealth management problem is just the vehicle. The skills transfer to any domain where you need to model decisions under uncertainty.

The Problem We Will Solve

Meet Robert, age 65, who just retired with $1.5 million split between a Traditional IRA and a taxable brokerage account. The IRS has two rules that pull in opposite directions:

Required Minimum Distributions (RMDs) Step-Up in Basis
Starting at age 73, Robert must withdraw money from his IRA every year. Each withdrawal is taxed as ordinary income. The government wants its tax revenue before he dies. When Robert dies, his daughter inherits his brokerage account at its current value. All the gains he never paid taxes on? Forgiven. She can sell immediately and owe nothing.

The question: Should Robert take money out early and pay taxes now, or hold everything and let his daughter inherit tax-free?

The answer depends on two things we cannot predict: how long Robert lives, and what the stock market does. If he dies at 70, holding wins. If he lives to 95, maybe not. We need to simulate thousands of possible futures to find out.

What You Will Build

Over 1.5 days, you will construct a simulation that:

  1. Generates 10,000 market scenarios using Geometric Brownian Motion
  2. Samples 10,000 possible lifespans from Social Security actuarial tables
  3. Applies tax rules to each scenario
  4. Stores results in DuckDB for analysis
  5. Finds the answer: When does Strategy A beat Strategy B?

How to Use These Materials

Work through the sessions in order. Each one builds on the previous:

Session What You Learn
1. The Problem Why this matters, the two tax rules in conflict, concrete scenarios with dollar amounts
2. Data Model How to represent tax lots, accounts, and transactions in a database schema
3. Why Simulate Why averages fail for retirement planning, the case for Monte Carlo
4. The Math Geometric Brownian Motion for markets, survival analysis for lifespans
5. Simulation Building the Monte Carlo engine, storing results in DuckDB
6. Analysis SQL queries to compute expected value, VaR, win rates, and decision boundaries

The Glossary is a reference. Use it when you encounter unfamiliar terms.

Hands-On Notebooks

Each session has a corresponding Jupyter notebook where you write code:

  1. Intro to Monte Carlo - Estimate pi by throwing darts
  2. Stock Price Simulation - Generate random market paths
  3. Survival Analysis - Sample lifespans from actuarial tables
  4. Tax Logic - Implement RMD and step-up rules
  5. Full Simulation - Put it all together with DuckDB

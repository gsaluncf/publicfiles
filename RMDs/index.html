Seminar Overview

Seminar Title: Stochastic Tax-Optimization & Portfolio Simulation

This 1.5-day seminar focuses on the intersection of distributed systems, database management, and probabilistic modeling within the context of wealth management. Students will build a simulation engine to quantify Tax Alpha—the value generated by optimizing financial decisions around Required Minimum Distributions (RMDs) and the "Step-up in Basis" tax rule.

The core problem: How do we maximize multi-generational wealth when market returns and human lifespans are uncertain? You will interact with a database of tax lots and run large-scale Monte Carlo simulations to model the trade-off between the tax penalties of RMDs and the tax benefits of holding appreciated assets until inheritance.

Learning Objectives

  • Probabilistic Modeling — Implement GBM for market paths; use survival analysis with actuarial life tables.
  • Database Architecture for Analytics — Design/query a tax-lot schema for thousands of simulated scenarios.
  • Monte Carlo Methods — Execute and aggregate 104+ paths to compute EV, VaR, and CIs.
  • Decision Science — Quantify the Tax Alpha of Hold-to-Death vs Aggressive Decumulation.

Technical Workflow

Phase Task Skill
Modeling Generate 10,000 market & lifespan paths Stochastic Processes & Survival Analysis
Processing Apply RMD and Step-up logic to tax-lot DB SQL / Logic Layer Integration
Analysis Aggregate to find the optimal strategy Statistical Inference & Distribution Analysis
Visualization Map the Tax‑Efficient Frontier Plotly / Seaborn / Matplotlib

Core Challenge

The primary goal is to find the point of indifference: at what combination of market volatility and life expectancy does it become more sound to pay RMD taxes early versus holding for the Step-up in Basis?

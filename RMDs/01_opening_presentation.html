Session 1: The Tax Alpha Problem

A Multi-Variable Optimization Challenge for Data Scientists

The Core Question

How do we maximize multi-generational wealth when market returns and human lifespans are both uncertain?

This seminar treats retirement wealth management as what it truly is: a stochastic optimization problem with competing tax strategies, probabilistic inputs, and no closed-form solution.

Why Data Scientists Should Care

You are not here to become financial advisors. You are here because this problem domain offers:

  • Real-world Monte Carlo application - Not a textbook example, but a problem where simulation is the only viable approach
  • Temporal database design - Financial data has time-varying attributes that break naive schemas
  • Decision science under uncertainty - Quantifying when Strategy A beats Strategy B across probability distributions
  • Multi-objective optimization - Balancing expected value against tail risk

The Backstory: Two Tax Rules in Tension

The U.S. tax code creates a fascinating optimization problem through two rules that pull in opposite directions:

Rule 1: Required Minimum Distributions (RMDs)

Starting at age 73, the IRS forces you to withdraw money from tax-deferred retirement accounts (401k, Traditional IRA). These withdrawals are taxed as ordinary income. The government wants its tax revenue before you die.

Rule 2: Step-Up in Basis

When you die, your heirs inherit your assets at their current market value, not what you paid. All unrealized capital gains disappear. The government forgives decades of appreciation.

The Tension

RMDs say: "Take money out now and pay taxes."

Step-Up says: "Hold until death and pay nothing."

Which strategy wins? It depends on how long you live and what the market does. Both are uncertain.

A Concrete Example

Consider a 65-year-old retiree with:

  • $1,000,000 in a Traditional IRA (all withdrawals taxed as income)
  • $500,000 in a taxable brokerage account (cost basis: $200,000, unrealized gain: $300,000)

Strategy A: Aggressive Decumulation

Convert IRA to Roth aggressively, pay taxes now at known rates, let Roth grow tax-free.

Strategy B: Hold to Death

Take only required RMDs, keep assets in taxable account, let heirs inherit with stepped-up basis.

If the retiree dies at 70, Strategy B wins decisively (heirs get step-up, avoided most RMDs).

If the retiree lives to 95, Strategy A may win (30 years of RMDs would have eroded the portfolio anyway).

The Data Science Question

At what combination of market volatility and life expectancy does the optimal strategy flip? This is not a calculation. This is a simulation.

What We Will Build

Over 1.5 days, you will construct a simulation engine that:

  1. Generates 10,000+ market paths using Geometric Brownian Motion
  2. Samples lifespans from actuarial survival distributions
  3. Applies tax logic to a database of individual tax lots
  4. Aggregates results to find expected values, confidence intervals, and decision boundaries
  5. Visualizes the tax-efficient frontier showing when each strategy dominates
